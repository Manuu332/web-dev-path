A Brief History

Dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) were the first animals domesticated by humans. Scientists believe this relationship began 15,000 to 30,000 years ago, when wolves started scavenging near human camps. Over time, these wolves evolved into dogs through selective breeding, becoming more friendly and useful to humans.
Dogs played essential roles in early societies:

  • Hunting Partners
    Helping track and retrieve game.
  • Guardians
    Protecting homes and livestock.
  • Workers
    Pulling sleds, herding sheep and assisting in farming.
Today, dogs continue to serve in many roles, including therapy, police work, search and rescue and as beloved family pets.
